# Comprehensive Linux Forensics Learning Resources

## Books

### Foundational & Comprehensive

1. **"The Art of Memory Forensics: Detecting Malware and Threats in Windows, Linux, and Mac Memory"** by Michael Hale Ligh, Andrew Case, Jamie Levy, Aaron Walters
    
    - Deep dive into memory forensics techniques
    - Covers Volatility framework extensively
    - Advanced content for serious practitioners
2. **"Linux Forensics: What You Know is What You Get"** by Matt Bromiley & Rick Ware
    
    - Practical Linux forensics methodology
    - Real-world incident response scenarios
    - Excellent for both beginners and professionals
3. **"Hacking Exposed: Industrial Control Systems"** by Andrew Colarik & Byron Norwood
    
    - Covers forensics in ICS/SCADA systems
    - Linux systems in industrial environments
4. **"Forensic Discovery"** by Dan Farmer & Wietse Venema
    
    - Classic text on discovering evidence
    - Still highly relevant despite age
    - Covers hidden files and rootkits
5. **"Incident Response & Computer Forensics"** by Jason T. Liston
    
    - Complete IR/forensics workflow
    - Windows and Linux coverage
    - Practical tools and techniques
6. **"A Practical Introduction to Digital Forensics"** by David B. Cowen & Joseph Cowen
    
    - Beginner-friendly approach
    - Covers multiple OSes including Linux
    - Hands-on exercises

### Advanced Topics

7. **"The Linux Programming Interface"** by Michael Kerrisk
    
    - Deep understanding of Linux internals
    - Essential for advanced forensics understanding
    - System calls and kernel concepts
8. **"Understanding the Linux Kernel"** by Daniel P. Bovet & Marco Cesati
    
    - Kernel architecture and internals
    - Critical for understanding forensic artifacts
9. **"The Hacker Playbook 3: Practical Guide To Penetration Testing"** by Peter Kim
    
    - Includes forensics post-compromise
    - Linux attack and evidence perspectives
10. **"Rootkits and Bootkits: Reversing Modern Malware and Next Generation Threats"** by Alex Matrosov, Eugene Rodionov & Sergey Bratus
    
    - Rootkit detection and analysis
    - Linux kernel-level malware

## Certifications Related to Linux Forensics

### Industry-Recognized Certs

1. **CompTIA Security+**
    
    - Foundation credential
    - Covers forensics basics
    - Widely recognized
    - Exam fee: ~$400
2. **Certified Incident Handler (eCIH)** - eLearnSecurity
    
    - Forensics-focused
    - Practical exam
    - Affordable ($600-800)
3. **Certified Forensic Computer Examiner (CFCE)**
    
    - EC-Council
    - Comprehensive forensics training
    - Mid-range cost (~$1,000-1,500)
4. **GIAC Certified Incident Handler (GCIH)** - SANS
    
    - Incident response and forensics
    - Industry gold standard
    - High cost ($7,000+ course)
5. **Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)** - EC-Council
    
    - Broader cert with forensics modules
    - Practical skills
    - ~$1,000-1,200
6. **GIAC Certified Forensic Examiner (GCFE)** - SANS
    
    - Advanced forensics certification
    - Expensive but highly valued
    - Requires course attendance

## Recommended Learning Path

1. **Start Here**: Linux fundamentals (NetworkChuck, Professor Messer)
2. **Foundation**: SANS Cyber Aces free tutorials
3. **Hands-On**: TryHackMe Linux forensics rooms
4. **Deep Dive**: 13cubed YouTube channel
5. **Practice**: HackTheBox and CTFLearn challenges
6. **Advanced**: "Linux Forensics: What You Know is What You Get" book

This comprehensive list provides multiple pathways into Linux forensics, whether you prefer video learning, reading, hands-on practice, or formal certifications. Start with free resources to test your interest, then invest in paid courses and certifications as your expertise grows.
